Read Before You Come

Before embarking on your volunteer journey with Sar-El Israel, please take note of the following important information:

  • Ensure you have a valid passport with at least six months validity beyond your planned stay.
  • Check visa requirements based on your nationality.
  • Familiarize yourself with local customs and cultural norms.
  • Pack appropriate clothing for both work and leisure.
  • Bring any necessary medications and a basic first-aid kit.
  • Be prepared for potentially challenging emotional and physical conditions.

Important Registration Information

In order to participate in the Sar-El volunteer experience, there are two mandatory stages that must be completed to further the registration process:

  • First stage: Complete and submit the application form. After the Sar-El staff reviews the application, it will go into a queue for a video interview. This gives us the chance to get to know you and to answer any questions you have regarding the program.
  • Second stage: Upon approval, you will receive an email with additional documents to be completed, including a medical form and an indemnification waiver.

The application process can take up to 6 to 8 weeks, so your patience is greatly appreciated. Health and travel insurance that covers your stay in the program is mandatory.

Please contact your local representative if you have additional questions regarding the application procedure. For the International Contacts page, click here.

Before You Begin the Registration Process

Before you begin the registration process, please make sure you meet the conditions of entry into Israel, as indicated on the Israeli Ministry of Health website. Click here for the latest guidelines.

Note: Do not buy an airline ticket until you have received confirmation of acceptance into the program.
